# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary:
- Military veterans considering or preparing for higher education, especially at elite institutions
- Academic advisors, mentors, and transition counselors who work with veterans
- University admissions officers interested in veteran pipeline programs
- Friends, family, and supporters of veterans in transition

Secondary:
- Literary readers and poetry enthusiasts discovering Nestor's work
- Environmental thinkers and academics engaging with his essays
- Event organizers and universities seeking speakers on veteran transition, education, or environmental ethics

Context:
Visitors arrive through organic discovery, referrals, or intentional search. They are emotionally intelligent, intellectually curious, and seeking depth over polish. Many have military backgrounds or academic ambitions. They are not browsing a product catalog; they are entering a lived narrative.

## Product Purpose

Sword Circle Pen exists to:
1. Archive and present Nestor Walters' literary and intellectual work (fiction, poetry, essays, environmental thought)
2. Communicate the Veteran Transfer Project's mission: helping veterans navigate admission to elite academic institutions
3. Inspire contemplation around service, education, identity, and environmental responsibility
4. Position Nestor as a public intellectual, speaker, and advocate

Success means the visitor leaves feeling emotionally moved, intellectually curious, and connected to a larger mission — not merely informed.

## Brand Personality

Contemplative, literary, cinematic, restrained, intelligent, authentic.

Three words: **atmospheric**, **intellectual**, **human**.

Emotional goals:
- Evoke the feeling of entering memory or discovering a journal
- Balance military discipline with scholarly introspection
- Communicate transformation without motivational clichés
- Maintain quiet dignity in every interaction

## Anti-references

- SaaS startup landing pages (big numbers, gradient accents, feature grids)
- Generic portfolio sites (boxed layouts, identical card grids, social-media aesthetics)
- Traditional author websites (static bio + book list)
- Corporate dashboards or admin UIs
- Overly bright or trendy social-media design
- Excessive glassmorphism or loud gradients
- Motivational clichés and "personal brand" language

## Design Principles

1. **Design is memory, not information.** Every surface should feel like entering an archive, not reading a brochure.
2. **Emotion over efficiency.** Pacing, atmosphere, and emotional transitions matter more than conversion optimization.
3. **Restraint is craft.** Avoid decorative excess. Every element earns its place through narrative purpose.
4. **Typography is storytelling.** Type choices carry emotional weight; treat them as narrative devices, not mere legibility.
5. **Motion serves memory.** Animations should feel like wind, breath, or drifting paper — never bounce, flash, or gimmick.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: disable parallax and heavy motion for users who need it
- Ensure sufficient color contrast despite the dark, atmospheric palette
- Keyboard-navigable interactive elements
- Semantic HTML for screen reader compatibility
- Consider veteran users who may have PTSD triggers: avoid sudden flashes, loud sounds, or aggressive motion
